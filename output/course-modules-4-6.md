### TEACH (10 minutes)

**[SLIDE: "The Cost of Silence" -- stats on screen]**

First, let us talk about what inconsistency is costing you right now.

Companies that post consistently generate 67 percent more leads per month. Email marketing delivers 36 dollars for every dollar spent. But here is the number that should bother you: when you post a burst of content one week and then go silent for three weeks, you are training your audience to forget you. The algorithms punish that. Your followers disengage. Open rates decay. And every single day you are not visible, a competitor fills that gap.

The old solution? Hire a content team. Social media manager -- 55 to 75K. Copywriter -- 50 to 70K. Video editor -- 45 to 65K. You are looking at 150 to 210 thousand dollars a year in salary alone. Plus benefits. Plus management overhead. Plus the three months it takes to onboard them to your voice.

The new solution costs you one hour per week and about 200 dollars a month.

**[SLIDE: "The Content Creation Engine -- SOP-002" with the framework diagram]**

Here is the framework. One Pillar recording per week. That is a 20 to 45 minute video or audio where you talk about what you already know. Not something you have to research. Something a client asked you this week. A mistake you see people making. A process you have refined.

From that single recording, AI generates:

**[SLIDE: The 30+ Content Pieces list -- animate each bullet]**

- One newsletter edition
- Five to seven social media posts, optimized per platform
- One email sequence of three to five emails
- One long-form blog post, 1,500 to 2,500 words
- Eight to twelve micro-content clips, 15 to 60 seconds each
- One set of quote graphics

That is 30-plus pieces. Enough to post twice a day across all your platforms for two full weeks. From one sitting.

**[SLIDE: "Step-by-Step Breakdown" header]**

Let me walk you through the six steps.

**[SLIDE: Step 1 -- Record Your Pillar Content]**

Step 1: Record your Pillar content. 30 to 45 minutes. Pick one topic. Use a three to five bullet outline -- do not script it word for word. Your audience wants your real perspective, not polished corporate copy. Record on your phone with a tripod, use Zoom, or if you want higher quality, Riverside.fm or Descript. Record both video and audio -- video gives you the most repurposing options.

**[SLIDE: Step 2 -- Transcribe and Extract]**

Step 2: Transcribe and extract. Upload to Descript, Otter.ai, or any transcription tool. Then feed that transcript into your LLM -- Claude, ChatGPT, or both. Use this prompt -- and I want you to save this prompt because you will use it every single week:

**[SLIDE: Show the master prompt on screen]**

"You are a content strategist. I am going to give you a transcript of a 30-minute recording. Extract: the three to five main points, the single most compelling insight, any quotable one-liners, a suggested blog post outline, seven social media post angles, and a three-email sequence outline. Audience: describe your audience. Tone: describe your voice."

That prompt does the heavy lifting. Save it. Customize it for your business. Use it every week.

**[SLIDE: Step 3 -- Generate Your Content Stack]**

Step 3: Generate your content stack. Blog post -- have the AI write a full 1,500 to 2,500 word post using your transcript as source material. This is not generic AI content. It is YOUR content, structured by AI. Social media posts -- generate five to seven posts optimized per platform. LinkedIn gets longer story-driven posts. Instagram gets punchy captions. X gets single-insight threads. TikTok gets short-form video scripts.

Newsletter -- pull the core insight, add a personal intro, include a CTA. It should feel like a note from a friend. Email sequence -- three to five emails that feed into your lead nurture system. Micro-content -- use Descript or OpusClip to auto-detect the best 15 to 60 second clips from your recording. AI finds the moments with the most energy and the clearest insights.

**[SLIDE: Step 4 -- "HUMAN REVIEW: NON-NEGOTIABLE" in bold red]**

Step 4: Human review. I need you to hear this clearly. AI drafts. Humans approve. Every single piece of content goes through a human review before it publishes. Period. This is not optional.

Why? Three reasons. One -- accuracy. AI hallucinate statistics. It misattributes quotes. It confidently states things that are wrong. Your reputation is on the line. Two -- voice. AI gets close, but close is not good enough. You need to read every piece and ask: would I actually say this? Three -- legal liability. Unsubstantiated claims about results or income create real legal exposure. A five-minute review catches problems a lawsuit will not forgive.

Block 60 to 90 minutes after your content stack is generated. Read everything. Edit what needs editing. Kill what does not sound like you. Approve the rest.

**[SLIDE: Step 5 -- Schedule and Distribute]**

Step 5: Schedule and distribute. Load your approved content into Buffer, Hootsuite, or Later. Two posts per day across your active platforms. Set your email sequences in your CRM -- ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, GoHighLevel. Upload your blog post. Optimize for SEO with SurferSEO or Clearscope if you want organic traffic.

**[SLIDE: Step 6 -- Measure and Iterate]**

Step 6: Measure and iterate. At the end of each two-week cycle, look at what performed. Which posts got the most engagement? Which emails had the highest open rates? Feed those insights back into your next Pillar recording. The system gets smarter every cycle because you are getting smarter about what your audience wants.

**[SLIDE: Tool Stack with monthly costs]**

Quick note on tools. Your total monthly cost: 100 to 200 dollars a month. Recording with Riverside at 15 a month. Transcription with Descript at 24. AI with Claude Pro at 20. Canva Pro at 13. Buffer at 6 per channel. OpusClip at 19 if you want auto-clips. Compare that to one content hire.

---

### TEACH (10 minutes)

**[SLIDE: "Creative Is the New Targeting"]**

First, you need to understand what changed. Privacy updates -- iOS 14.5, cookie deprecation -- broke the old targeting playbook. The platforms' algorithms now reward creative quality over audience targeting. Meta's own data shows creative quality accounts for 56 percent of auction outcomes. Up from 30 percent just three years ago.

Translation: the ad that wins is not the one shown to the perfect audience. It is the one that stops the scroll.

The good news? Creating high-performing ad creative used to cost 5,000 to 15,000 dollars per video. Now, with AI, you can generate 12 ad variants for the cost of lunch. Test them all for 20 to 50 dollars a day. And have statistically significant data within 72 hours.

**[SLIDE: "The New Standard Ad Framework" with the three sections diagrammed]**

Here is the framework. Every high-converting short-form ad follows the same architecture. We did not invent this. We studied thousands of winning ads and reverse-engineered the pattern.

**[SLIDE: Section 1 -- Thumb-Stopper Hook, animated]**

Section 1: The Thumb-Stopper Hook. Zero to seven seconds. The only job of the first six to seven seconds is to stop the scroll. You are not selling yet. You are not explaining. You are interrupting a pattern.

Effective hooks fall into four categories:

Contrarian statement -- "Everything your marketing agency told you about Facebook ads is wrong."

Specific result -- "We generated 47 qualified leads in 9 days for 312 dollars total ad spend."

Direct question -- "How much did you spend on ads last month with nothing to show for it?"

Pattern interrupt -- unexpected visuals, movement, or text that break the scroll behavior.

**[SLIDE: Section 2 -- Problem Agitation]**

Section 2: Problem Agitation. Seven to twenty seconds. Now that they have stopped, agitate the problem they already know they have. Do not introduce new problems. Amplify existing pain.

"You keep hiring marketing people who promise the world and deliver a Canva graphic once a week. Your website gets traffic but nobody fills out the form. You have tried three agencies and you still cannot tell which channel is actually working."

Key rule: use THEIR language, not yours. If your customers say "I am burning money on ads," do not say "inefficient media allocation." Say "burning money."

**[SLIDE: Section 3 -- Solution Proof + CTA]**

Section 3: Solution Proof plus CTA. Twenty to forty-five seconds. Show the solution working. Not a pitch deck. Not a features list. Proof. A specific case study, a before-and-after, a real result.

End with a clear, single CTA. Not "learn more." Not "visit our website." Something specific: "Book a free 15-minute audit." Or "Download the framework." Or "Get your free AI chatbot setup."

Total runtime: 30 to 45 seconds. That is the sweet spot for Meta and TikTok.

**[SLIDE: "The Ad Creative Matrix" -- 3x3 grid visual]**

Now, before you write a single ad, you need to build your Ad Creative Matrix. This is a grid with three axes:

Problem -- what specific pain point are you addressing? List three to five.

Motivation -- what is driving them to solve it NOW? List three to five.

Desire -- what outcome do they actually want? List three to five.

Cross-reference them. Five problems times five motivations times five desires equals 125 possible ad angles. You do not need 125 ads. But you need to test at least 8 to 12 before you know what resonates.

**[SLIDE: "Multitude of Counselors" -- logos of Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini]**

Here is where AI changes the game. Take your top three to four angles from the matrix and feed them to your LLM using the New Standard Framework structure. But -- and this is important -- do not use just one AI.

I call this the "Multitude of Counselors" approach. Get ad concepts from Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini. Compare the outputs. Cherry-pick the best hooks, the sharpest agitation, the most compelling CTAs. Combine them into your final scripts.

Why? Each model has different creative strengths. Claude tends to write more structured, logical copy. ChatGPT often produces more conversational, emotionally resonant hooks. Gemini sometimes surfaces unexpected angles. Cross-referencing before you spend money is free insurance.

**[SLIDE: "Three Production Tiers"]**

You have three production tiers:

Tier 1 -- Talking Head. Free to 50 bucks. Record yourself on your phone. Add captions with Descript or CapCut. This is often the highest-converting format because it feels real.

Tier 2 -- AI-Enhanced. 50 to 200 bucks. ElevenLabs for voiceover. Canva for motion graphics. AI-generated B-roll footage.

Tier 3 -- Hybrid. 200 to 500 bucks. Combine talking head with AI B-roll, professional voiceover sections, and motion graphics for data points. This is the production quality of a 5,000 dollar agency ad at a fraction of the cost.

For each angle, produce at least three creative variants. Different hooks on the same body. Different visuals. Different CTAs.

**[SLIDE: "Kill Rules" -- in bold, large font]**

Now the most important part of this entire module. The kill rules.

Launch your ads at 20 to 50 dollars a day per variant. This is test budget -- you are buying data, not results.

The kill rules are non-negotiable:

72 hours with CTR below 0.7 percent? Kill the ad.

72 hours with zero leads? Kill the ad.

Cost per lead above 3x your target? Kill the ad.

No emotions. No "but I really liked that one." No "let us give it another week." The data speaks. You listen.

The ads that survive get scaled. Increase budget 20 to 30 percent every three days on winners. Let the losers die fast so the winners can eat.

**[SLIDE: "Scaling Winners"]**

Once you have a winner -- CTR above 1.5 percent, cost per lead at or below target, positive ROAS within seven days -- scale methodically. Increase daily budget 20 to 30 percent every three to four days. Duplicate the winner into new ad sets targeting different audiences. Create three to five fresh variants using the same winning angle. Test the same script on different platforms. A single winning angle can run profitably for four to eight weeks before creative fatigue sets in.

### TEACH (11 minutes)

**[SLIDE: "The Real Revenue Leak"]**

Before we build the system, let me show you the real problem. It is not that you do not get enough leads. It is that you do not follow up fast enough, consistently enough, or long enough.

Harvard Business Review found that companies responding to a new lead within five minutes are 100x more likely to make contact and 21x more likely to qualify that lead compared to companies that wait 30 minutes. Five minutes. Not five hours. Not "next business day."

And here is the other stat: 80 percent of sales require five or more follow-ups, but 44 percent of salespeople give up after one. Nearly half your sales team quits after one attempt when the data says you need five.

This is not a people problem. It is a systems problem. Humans are terrible at consistent follow-up. AI does not have bad days. AI does not forget. AI follows up at 7 AM on Saturday when your competitor's sales team is sleeping in.

**[SLIDE: "Step 1: Define Your ICP with Psychographics"]**

Step 1: Define your Ideal Customer Profile using psychographics, not demographics.

Most businesses define their ICP with demographics: business owners, 45 to 60, 1 to 10 million revenue, service-based. That is a starting point, but it is not enough. Demographics tell you who they are. Psychographics tell you when they will buy.

Your ICP should be defined by pain-point keywords -- the specific problems that make someone ready to act right now:

**[SLIDE: Pain Point Examples -- bullet list]**

"I cannot find good employees."
"We are growing but margins are shrinking."
"I am spending money on marketing but I cannot tell what is working."
"My competitor just launched something new."
"I am working 70 hours a week and nothing is changing."

These are buying triggers. When someone is experiencing one of these, they are not browsing -- they are searching. Your job is to be the solution they find at that exact moment.

Build a Pain Point Library: 15 to 20 specific phrases your ideal customer would say, type into Google, or post about on LinkedIn. This library drives your content from Module 4, your ads from Module 5, and now your prospecting.

**[SLIDE: "Step 2: The Free Widget Trojan Horse"]**

Step 2: Value-first outreach. Let me introduce you to the Free Widget Trojan Horse.

Cold outreach says: "Hi, I am CJ, I sell AI solutions, want to hop on a call?"

Value-first outreach says: "Hi, I noticed your website does not have a chat widget. You are probably losing 15 to 20 percent of traffic that would have engaged if they could ask a quick question. I built one for a similar business last month and it generated 23 new leads in two weeks. Want me to set one up for you for free?"

Why does this work? Zero risk for the prospect -- it is free. Immediate tangible value -- they see it working. Foot in the door -- once you solve one problem, they ask about others. Proof of competence -- you demonstrated, not just claimed.

The free widget converts to a paid engagement 30 to 40 percent of the time within 60 days. Because once they see leads coming in from something you built, the conversation shifts from "convince me" to "what else can you do?"

Use AI to personalize at scale. Feed a prospect's website URL into Claude and ask it to identify three specific problems and draft a personalized outreach message. What used to take 20 minutes per prospect now takes 30 seconds.

**[SLIDE: "Step 3: 24/7 Lead Capture"]**

Step 3: Deploy 24/7 lead capture. Your website and social profiles should never sleep.

Set up AI-powered chatbots that greet visitors within three seconds. They ask qualifying questions. They route qualified leads to your calendar. They capture email and phone for leads not ready to book yet.

**[SLIDE: The 4-Question Qualification Framework]**

Here is the four-question qualification framework your chatbot needs:

Question 1: "What is the biggest challenge you are facing with [your area] right now?" -- that is pain identification.

Question 2: "How long has this been an issue?" -- urgency gauge.

Question 3: "Have you tried solving this before? What happened?" -- solution awareness.

Question 4: "If we could solve this in the next 30 to 60 days, would that be worth a conversation?" -- intent confirmation.

Anyone who answers all four positively gets routed to your calendar. Everyone else goes into your nurture sequence.

**[SLIDE: "Step 4: The Revenue Ladder Funnel" -- visual ladder diagram]**

Step 4: Build your Revenue Ladder funnel. Not every lead is ready to spend 5,000 dollars on day one. Your funnel meets them where they are and moves them up.

**[SLIDE: Rung 1 -- Free Lead Magnet]**

Rung 1: Free Lead Magnet. Zero dollars. A checklist, audit template, or mini-course that solves one specific, narrow problem. Something like "The 5-Minute AI Audit: Find Out If Your Business Is Leaving Money on the Table." This captures email addresses and starts the relationship.

**[SLIDE: Rung 2 -- 7-Day Nurture]**

Rung 2: Seven-day micro-nurture email sequence. Automated. Day 1: deliver the lead magnet, introduce yourself. Day 2: relevant case study. Day 3: address the number one objection. Day 4: unexpected value -- a tool, a template, a shortcut. Day 5: social proof. Day 6: create urgency. Day 7: direct CTA to book or buy.

AI writes the first drafts. Human reviews and approves. The sequence runs automatically forever once it is set up.

**[SLIDE: Rung 3 -- Tripwire]**

Rung 3: Tripwire offer. 97 to 197 dollars. A low-cost, high-value product. An assessment, a template bundle, a recorded workshop. The goal is not profit -- it is to identify buyers. Someone who spends 97 dollars is 10x more likely to spend 2,500 than someone who only downloaded a freebie.

**[SLIDE: Rung 4 -- Core Offer]**

Rung 4: Core offer. 1,497 to 2,497 dollars. Your main product or service. Done-with-you implementation. Consulting engagement. Software setup. This is where the real revenue lives.

**[SLIDE: Rung 5 -- Retainer]**

Rung 5: Retainer or premium offer. 5,000 to 25,000 dollars per month. Ongoing service, managed AI systems, fractional CMO arrangement. This transforms your business from project-based to recurring revenue.

**[SLIDE: "Step 5: UTM Governance" -- UTM structure breakdown]**

Step 5: UTM governance. This is the unsexy step that separates professionals from amateurs.

Every link you share -- every ad, every email, every social post, every chatbot CTA -- must have UTM parameters. No exceptions.

utm_source: where the traffic comes from. Facebook, LinkedIn, email, Google.
utm_medium: what type of content. Paid, organic, newsletter, chatbot.
utm_campaign: which specific campaign. Spring audit offer, free widget outreach.
utm_content: which specific creative variant. Hook A, hook B, testimonial video.

Without UTM tracking, you are making decisions blind. You will think "Facebook is working" when actually it is one specific ad to one specific audience with one specific hook -- and the other nine campaigns are losing money.

The difference between "we spent 10,000 on marketing" and "we spent 3,200 on LinkedIn ads that generated 47 leads at 68 dollars each with a 23 percent close rate" is the difference between gambling and investing.

**[SLIDE: "Step 6: Measure Weekly"]**

Step 6: Measure weekly. Track lead volume by source, cost per lead by source, lead-to-meeting conversion rate, meeting-to-close rate, and revenue per lead by source.

Feed data back. Double down on sources that produce leads that close. Cut sources that produce leads that waste time. The system compounds. Month 1, setup. Month 2, tuning. Month 3, scaling. By Month 6, your pipeline fills itself and your only job is showing up to the meetings your system booked.
