### TEACH (10 minutes)

**[SLIDE: "Creative Is the New Targeting"]**

First, you need to understand what changed. Privacy updates -- iOS 14.5, cookie deprecation -- broke the old targeting playbook. The platforms' algorithms now reward creative quality over audience targeting. Meta's own data shows creative quality accounts for 56 percent of auction outcomes. Up from 30 percent just three years ago.

Translation: the ad that wins is not the one shown to the perfect audience. It is the one that stops the scroll.

The good news? Creating high-performing ad creative used to cost 5,000 to 15,000 dollars per video. Now, with AI, you can generate 12 ad variants for the cost of lunch. Test them all for 20 to 50 dollars a day. And have statistically significant data within 72 hours.

**[SLIDE: "The New Standard Ad Framework" with the three sections diagrammed]**

Here is the framework. Every high-converting short-form ad follows the same architecture. We did not invent this. We studied thousands of winning ads and reverse-engineered the pattern.

**[SLIDE: Section 1 -- Thumb-Stopper Hook, animated]**

Section 1: The Thumb-Stopper Hook. Zero to seven seconds. The only job of the first six to seven seconds is to stop the scroll. You are not selling yet. You are not explaining. You are interrupting a pattern.

Effective hooks fall into four categories:

Contrarian statement -- "Everything your marketing agency told you about Facebook ads is wrong."

Specific result -- "We generated 47 qualified leads in 9 days for 312 dollars total ad spend."

Direct question -- "How much did you spend on ads last month with nothing to show for it?"

Pattern interrupt -- unexpected visuals, movement, or text that break the scroll behavior.

**[SLIDE: Section 2 -- Problem Agitation]**

Section 2: Problem Agitation. Seven to twenty seconds. Now that they have stopped, agitate the problem they already know they have. Do not introduce new problems. Amplify existing pain.

"You keep hiring marketing people who promise the world and deliver a Canva graphic once a week. Your website gets traffic but nobody fills out the form. You have tried three agencies and you still cannot tell which channel is actually working."

Key rule: use THEIR language, not yours. If your customers say "I am burning money on ads," do not say "inefficient media allocation." Say "burning money."

**[SLIDE: Section 3 -- Solution Proof + CTA]**

Section 3: Solution Proof plus CTA. Twenty to forty-five seconds. Show the solution working. Not a pitch deck. Not a features list. Proof. A specific case study, a before-and-after, a real result.

End with a clear, single CTA. Not "learn more." Not "visit our website." Something specific: "Book a free 15-minute audit." Or "Download the framework." Or "Get your free AI chatbot setup."

Total runtime: 30 to 45 seconds. That is the sweet spot for Meta and TikTok.

**[SLIDE: "The Ad Creative Matrix" -- 3x3 grid visual]**

Now, before you write a single ad, you need to build your Ad Creative Matrix. This is a grid with three axes:

Problem -- what specific pain point are you addressing? List three to five.

Motivation -- what is driving them to solve it NOW? List three to five.

Desire -- what outcome do they actually want? List three to five.

Cross-reference them. Five problems times five motivations times five desires equals 125 possible ad angles. You do not need 125 ads. But you need to test at least 8 to 12 before you know what resonates.

**[SLIDE: "Multitude of Counselors" -- logos of Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini]**

Here is where AI changes the game. Take your top three to four angles from the matrix and feed them to your LLM using the New Standard Framework structure. But -- and this is important -- do not use just one AI.

I call this the "Multitude of Counselors" approach. Get ad concepts from Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini. Compare the outputs. Cherry-pick the best hooks, the sharpest agitation, the most compelling CTAs. Combine them into your final scripts.

Why? Each model has different creative strengths. Claude tends to write more structured, logical copy. ChatGPT often produces more conversational, emotionally resonant hooks. Gemini sometimes surfaces unexpected angles. Cross-referencing before you spend money is free insurance.

**[SLIDE: "Three Production Tiers"]**

You have three production tiers:

Tier 1 -- Talking Head. Free to 50 bucks. Record yourself on your phone. Add captions with Descript or CapCut. This is often the highest-converting format because it feels real.

Tier 2 -- AI-Enhanced. 50 to 200 bucks. ElevenLabs for voiceover. Canva for motion graphics. AI-generated B-roll footage.

Tier 3 -- Hybrid. 200 to 500 bucks. Combine talking head with AI B-roll, professional voiceover sections, and motion graphics for data points. This is the production quality of a 5,000 dollar agency ad at a fraction of the cost.

For each angle, produce at least three creative variants. Different hooks on the same body. Different visuals. Different CTAs.

**[SLIDE: "Kill Rules" -- in bold, large font]**

Now the most important part of this entire module. The kill rules.

Launch your ads at 20 to 50 dollars a day per variant. This is test budget -- you are buying data, not results.

The kill rules are non-negotiable:

72 hours with CTR below 0.7 percent? Kill the ad.

72 hours with zero leads? Kill the ad.

Cost per lead above 3x your target? Kill the ad.

No emotions. No "but I really liked that one." No "let us give it another week." The data speaks. You listen.

The ads that survive get scaled. Increase budget 20 to 30 percent every three days on winners. Let the losers die fast so the winners can eat.

**[SLIDE: "Scaling Winners"]**

Once you have a winner -- CTR above 1.5 percent, cost per lead at or below target, positive ROAS within seven days -- scale methodically. Increase daily budget 20 to 30 percent every three to four days. Duplicate the winner into new ad sets targeting different audiences. Create three to five fresh variants using the same winning angle. Test the same script on different platforms. A single winning angle can run profitably for four to eight weeks before creative fatigue sets in.

**Question 1:** According to the New Standard Ad Framework, what is the ONLY job of the first 6-7 seconds of an ad?

A) Explain your product or service clearly
B) Present social proof and testimonials
C) Stop the scroll -- interrupt the viewer's pattern
D) Deliver your call to action while you have attention

**Correct Answer: C** -- The Thumb-Stopper Hook exists solely to stop the scroll. You are not selling, explaining, or pitching in the first 6-7 seconds. You are interrupting a pattern so the viewer pauses and watches the rest.
