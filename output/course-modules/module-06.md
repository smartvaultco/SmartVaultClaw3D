### TEACH (11 minutes)

**[SLIDE: "The Real Revenue Leak"]**

Before we build the system, let me show you the real problem. It is not that you do not get enough leads. It is that you do not follow up fast enough, consistently enough, or long enough.

Harvard Business Review found that companies responding to a new lead within five minutes are 100x more likely to make contact and 21x more likely to qualify that lead compared to companies that wait 30 minutes. Five minutes. Not five hours. Not "next business day."

And here is the other stat: 80 percent of sales require five or more follow-ups, but 44 percent of salespeople give up after one. Nearly half your sales team quits after one attempt when the data says you need five.

This is not a people problem. It is a systems problem. Humans are terrible at consistent follow-up. AI does not have bad days. AI does not forget. AI follows up at 7 AM on Saturday when your competitor's sales team is sleeping in.

**[SLIDE: "Step 1: Define Your ICP with Psychographics"]**

Step 1: Define your Ideal Customer Profile using psychographics, not demographics.

Most businesses define their ICP with demographics: business owners, 45 to 60, 1 to 10 million revenue, service-based. That is a starting point, but it is not enough. Demographics tell you who they are. Psychographics tell you when they will buy.

Your ICP should be defined by pain-point keywords -- the specific problems that make someone ready to act right now:

**[SLIDE: Pain Point Examples -- bullet list]**

"I cannot find good employees."
"We are growing but margins are shrinking."
"I am spending money on marketing but I cannot tell what is working."
"My competitor just launched something new."
"I am working 70 hours a week and nothing is changing."

These are buying triggers. When someone is experiencing one of these, they are not browsing -- they are searching. Your job is to be the solution they find at that exact moment.

Build a Pain Point Library: 15 to 20 specific phrases your ideal customer would say, type into Google, or post about on LinkedIn. This library drives your content from Module 4, your ads from Module 5, and now your prospecting.

**[SLIDE: "Step 2: The Free Widget Trojan Horse"]**

Step 2: Value-first outreach. Let me introduce you to the Free Widget Trojan Horse.

Cold outreach says: "Hi, I am CJ, I sell AI solutions, want to hop on a call?"

Value-first outreach says: "Hi, I noticed your website does not have a chat widget. You are probably losing 15 to 20 percent of traffic that would have engaged if they could ask a quick question. I built one for a similar business last month and it generated 23 new leads in two weeks. Want me to set one up for you for free?"

Why does this work? Zero risk for the prospect -- it is free. Immediate tangible value -- they see it working. Foot in the door -- once you solve one problem, they ask about others. Proof of competence -- you demonstrated, not just claimed.

The free widget converts to a paid engagement 30 to 40 percent of the time within 60 days. Because once they see leads coming in from something you built, the conversation shifts from "convince me" to "what else can you do?"

Use AI to personalize at scale. Feed a prospect's website URL into Claude and ask it to identify three specific problems and draft a personalized outreach message. What used to take 20 minutes per prospect now takes 30 seconds.

**[SLIDE: "Step 3: 24/7 Lead Capture"]**

Step 3: Deploy 24/7 lead capture. Your website and social profiles should never sleep.

Set up AI-powered chatbots that greet visitors within three seconds. They ask qualifying questions. They route qualified leads to your calendar. They capture email and phone for leads not ready to book yet.

**[SLIDE: The 4-Question Qualification Framework]**

Here is the four-question qualification framework your chatbot needs:

Question 1: "What is the biggest challenge you are facing with [your area] right now?" -- that is pain identification.

Question 2: "How long has this been an issue?" -- urgency gauge.

Question 3: "Have you tried solving this before? What happened?" -- solution awareness.

Question 4: "If we could solve this in the next 30 to 60 days, would that be worth a conversation?" -- intent confirmation.

Anyone who answers all four positively gets routed to your calendar. Everyone else goes into your nurture sequence.

**[SLIDE: "Step 4: The Revenue Ladder Funnel" -- visual ladder diagram]**

Step 4: Build your Revenue Ladder funnel. Not every lead is ready to spend 5,000 dollars on day one. Your funnel meets them where they are and moves them up.

**[SLIDE: Rung 1 -- Free Lead Magnet]**

Rung 1: Free Lead Magnet. Zero dollars. A checklist, audit template, or mini-course that solves one specific, narrow problem. Something like "The 5-Minute AI Audit: Find Out If Your Business Is Leaving Money on the Table." This captures email addresses and starts the relationship.

**[SLIDE: Rung 2 -- 7-Day Nurture]**

Rung 2: Seven-day micro-nurture email sequence. Automated. Day 1: deliver the lead magnet, introduce yourself. Day 2: relevant case study. Day 3: address the number one objection. Day 4: unexpected value -- a tool, a template, a shortcut. Day 5: social proof. Day 6: create urgency. Day 7: direct CTA to book or buy.

AI writes the first drafts. Human reviews and approves. The sequence runs automatically forever once it is set up.

**[SLIDE: Rung 3 -- Tripwire]**

Rung 3: Tripwire offer. 97 to 197 dollars. A low-cost, high-value product. An assessment, a template bundle, a recorded workshop. The goal is not profit -- it is to identify buyers. Someone who spends 97 dollars is 10x more likely to spend 2,500 than someone who only downloaded a freebie.

**[SLIDE: Rung 4 -- Core Offer]**

Rung 4: Core offer. 1,497 to 2,497 dollars. Your main product or service. Done-with-you implementation. Consulting engagement. Software setup. This is where the real revenue lives.

**[SLIDE: Rung 5 -- Retainer]**

Rung 5: Retainer or premium offer. 5,000 to 25,000 dollars per month. Ongoing service, managed AI systems, fractional CMO arrangement. This transforms your business from project-based to recurring revenue.

**[SLIDE: "Step 5: UTM Governance" -- UTM structure breakdown]**

Step 5: UTM governance. This is the unsexy step that separates professionals from amateurs.

Every link you share -- every ad, every email, every social post, every chatbot CTA -- must have UTM parameters. No exceptions.

utm_source: where the traffic comes from. Facebook, LinkedIn, email, Google.
utm_medium: what type of content. Paid, organic, newsletter, chatbot.
utm_campaign: which specific campaign. Spring audit offer, free widget outreach.
utm_content: which specific creative variant. Hook A, hook B, testimonial video.

Without UTM tracking, you are making decisions blind. You will think "Facebook is working" when actually it is one specific ad to one specific audience with one specific hook -- and the other nine campaigns are losing money.

The difference between "we spent 10,000 on marketing" and "we spent 3,200 on LinkedIn ads that generated 47 leads at 68 dollars each with a 23 percent close rate" is the difference between gambling and investing.

**[SLIDE: "Step 6: Measure Weekly"]**

Step 6: Measure weekly. Track lead volume by source, cost per lead by source, lead-to-meeting conversion rate, meeting-to-close rate, and revenue per lead by source.

Feed data back. Double down on sources that produce leads that close. Cut sources that produce leads that waste time. The system compounds. Month 1, setup. Month 2, tuning. Month 3, scaling. By Month 6, your pipeline fills itself and your only job is showing up to the meetings your system booked.
