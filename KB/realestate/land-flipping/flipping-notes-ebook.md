## CORE STRATEGY: NOTE FLIPPING FUNDAMENTALS

### What Is Note Flipping?
**Definition**: Flipping real estate promissory notes (not properties). You buy and sell the contractual loan agreements secured by real estate, not the physical properties themselves.

**Key Distinction**:
> You're flipping the NOTE not the house.

### Why Note Flipping Works

#### Market Size & Opportunity
- **Multi-trillion dollar industry**
- ~6% of all residential real estate sales involve seller financing
- **2014 Data**: 4.93 million home sales × $208,500 median price × 6% = **~$61.6 billion in seller-financed notes in one year alone**
- Growth trend: 2014 marked 5 consecutive years of growth with 4.7% increase over 2013 (Source: Advanced Seller Data Services)
- Notes also exist for: new homes, land, mobile homes, business notes

#### Advantages vs. Traditional Flipping

| Factor | Note Flipping | Traditional Flipping |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Competition** | Virtually unlimited pool; nobody else looking | Fighting over same deals |
| **Capital Required** | None needed | Significant down payment |
| **Ownership/Management** | None; no rental properties | Must own and manage |
| **Rehab Work** | None required | Must manage rehabs |
| **Credit Requirements** | None | Good credit needed |
| **Licensing/Experience** | None required | May require licenses |
| **Location Flexibility** | Deals in any market (remote) | Must see properties in person |
| **Work Location** | Anywhere (home-based) | Must be on-site |
| **Risk Level** | Lower | Higher |

### Who Should Flip Notes?

✅ **Note Flipping Is For You If You**:
- Want to make extra money part-time or supplement current income
- Looking for a new career path allowing remote work
- Are a real estate investor frustrated with deal scarcity (foreclosure inventory down 29% YoY per CoreLogic)
- Want quick money without traditional real estate hassles

## FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPTS: LEGAL DOCUMENTS & INSTRUMENTS

### The Two Critical Documents

#### 1. Promissory Note (The Promise to Repay)
**Definition**: A legal document representing a promise to repay a debt.

**Contains**: 
- Interest rate
- Due date
- Terms of the loan
- Repayment schedule

**Key Attribute**: Creates the cash flow/income stream

#### 2. Mortgage or Deed of Trust (The Collateral Pledge)
**Definition**: The legal instrument that pledges the property as collateral.

**Function**: 
- Secures the promissory note
- Allows lender to seize property if borrower defaults
- Differs by state (mortgage vs. deed of trust)

**Key Distinction**: If borrower defaults, note holder can foreclose and sell property to repay the loan

### Important Notes Distinction
> For the sake of this eBook and the note flipping strategy I outline herein, I am specifically referring to real estate loans from private individuals and/or entities and seller financed loans and NOT bank loans.

**NOT Bank Notes**: The strategy focuses on **private loans** and **seller-financed loans**, not traditional bank paper.

**Why**: 
- Bank loans are highly regulated and follow stringent processes
- Private loans can easily be bought, sold, and flipped
- No shortage of private loans available
