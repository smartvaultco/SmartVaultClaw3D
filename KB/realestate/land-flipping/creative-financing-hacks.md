## CORE FRAMEWORK: CREATIVE FINANCING OVERVIEW

### Two Deal Structuring Options

| Option | Method | Outcome |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Option 1 | Cash or traditional financing | Seller paid in full immediately; ownership transfers |
| Option 2 | Creative financing strategies | Alternative acquisition methods; deferred or partial payment; control without full capital |

### Fundamental Pricing Rule

```
CASH = Pay Less
TERMS = Pay More
```

**Key Insight**: Using creative financing eliminates the "expensive cost of cash," allowing investors to pay sellers MORE while requiring LESS capital upfront. This opens deals cash-only investors pass on.

**Mutual Benefit Model**:
- Seller: Earns more on terms + interest cash flow
- Buyer: Reduces capital requirement + increases deal flow

---

## PROCESS 1: TWO-STEP OFFER METHODOLOGY

### Step 1: Low All-Cash Offer
**Objective**: Establish floor price by offering fastest/most convenient exit

**Positioning**:
- Lead with "all-cash" + "close quickly"
- Frame as permanent removal from seller's life
- Accept lower sale price in exchange for speed

### Step 2: Counter with Creative Financing
**Objective**: Re-engage seller with higher price on flexible terms

**Positioning**: Offer to pay MORE if seller accepts deferred/creative payment structure

### Two-Step Messaging Framework

> "If you want to be rid of this property as fast as possible and you want your money now…here's my all-cash offer but if you're willing to wait to get paid and are willing to be flexible on terms, then I can pay you more for it."

### Seller Decision Framework
**Core Question for Seller**:
```
Less money NOW
or
More money LATER?
```

## CREATIVE FINANCING METHOD #1: SELLER FINANCING (Owner Carry-Back)

### Definition & Structure
- **AKA**: Owner financing, owner carry-back
- **Mechanism**: Seller becomes lender on their own property
- **Buyer Position**: Receives deed; pays via seller-financed loan
- **Seller Protection**: Mortgage lien on property; collects monthly interest payments
- **Ownership Transfer**: Occurs immediately (like traditional sale)

### Example Scenario
```
Property Purchase Price: $100,000
Buyer Down Payment: Little to none
Seller Finances: Balance as loan
Seller Receives: Monthly payments + interest
Seller Security: Mortgage lien on property
```

### CRITICAL ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENT
**Seller Financing ONLY works when**:
- Seller owns property FREE AND CLEAR
- No existing mortgage liens
- No existing lien holders

**If seller has existing mortgage**: Use "subject-to" strategy instead (see separate section)

### Market Data
Approximately 1/3 of all real estate in U.S. is owned free and clear (contradicts common assumption that "no one owns properties free and clear")

## SELLER FINANCING: TWO MOTIVATIONS

### Motivation #1: Higher Purchase Price

#### Cash Offer Formula
```
CASH BUY PRICE = (ARV × 70%) – Repair Costs
```

#### Seller Carry Offer Formula
```
SELLER CARRY BUY PRICE = (ARV × 85%) – Repair Costs
```

#### Comparison Example
**Given**:
- ARV: $100,000
- Repairs: $15,000

**Cash Offer**:
```
($100,000 × 70%) - $15,000 = $55,000 buy price
```

**Seller Carry Offer**:
```
($100,000 × 85%) - $15,000 = $70,000 buy price
```

**Price Differential**: $15,000 MORE for seller as incentive

### Motivation #2: Monthly Cash Flow (Interest Payments)
Seller earns recurring revenue stream from loan interest on monthly payments

## SELLER FINANCING: NEGOTIABLE TERMS

### Term #1: Down Payment

**Standard Range**: 5-10% (sellers want "skin in game" from buyer)

**Possible Down Payment**: 0% (rare, seller-dependent)

#### Down Payment Workaround: Rehab as Equity Investment

**Strategy**: Use planned rehabilitation costs as your "skin in the game" alternative

**Presentation Script**:
> "I'm going to spend [REPAIR AMOUNT] renovating the home, which will increase the equity or value of the home and also count as my skin in the deal."

**Enhanced Version**: Offer to place rehab funds in escrow at closing, to be used for repairs (increases seller comfort)

**Example**:
```
Purchase Price: $70,000
Down Payment: $0
Rehab Budget: $15,000 (in escrow)
Seller Perception: Buyer has $15,000 committed
```

### Term #2: Interest Rate

**Decision Tree**:

| Holding Strategy | Interest Rate Approach | Rationale |
|------------------|------------------------|-----------|
| Long-term hold | Negotiate LOW rate | Minimize long-term payments |
| Flip (quick resale) | Accept HIGHER rate | Hold property only months; interest cost minor vs. capital savings |

**Flip Strategy Example**:
- Instead of: 5% interest + 5-10% down payment
- Offer: 8% interest + $0 down
- **Benefit**: Pay higher % for shorter duration rather than tie up capital upfront

### Term #3: Loan Due Date (Balloon Payment)

**Definition**: Date loan must be paid off in full (not amortized over full loan term)

**Standard Amortization**: 30 years
**Typical Balloon Timeline**: 1-5 years (much shorter than amortization)

#### Decision Tree

| Holding Strategy | Balloon Negotiation | Rationale |
|------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Long-term hold | Negotiate LONGEST balloon possible | Extend payment timeline; reduce refinance urgency |
| Flip strategy | Accept SHORTER balloon | Plan to payoff via sale proceeds; timeline aligns with flip window |

## SELLER FINANCING: PRO TIP ACTIVATION SCRIPT

### Motivation Technique: Price Differentiation Education

**Trigger**: When you know seller owns property free and clear

**Sequence**:
1. Present low all-cash offer (establish baseline price)
2. Then transition with:

> "If you're willing to consider owner financing, I can pay you a much higher price to buy the home."

**Expected Seller Response**:
> "Well what does that look like?"

**Outcome**: Opens door to seller carry discussion and term negotiation

## FORMULAS & CALCULATIONS

### Buy Price Formula: Cash Offer
```
Buy Price = (ARV × 0.70) - Repairs
```

### Buy Price Formula: Seller Carry Offer
```
Buy Price = (ARV × 0.85) - Repairs
```

### Price Increase Differential
```
Additional Price Available = (ARV × 0.15) - (ARV × 0.70)
                           = ARV × 0.15
```

**Example** (ARV $100K):
```
$100,000 × 0.15 = $15,000 additional available for seller carry
```
