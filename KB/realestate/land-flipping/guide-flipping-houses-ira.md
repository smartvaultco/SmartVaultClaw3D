## TAX IMPLICATIONS & CRITICAL WARNINGS

### Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT)

**Rule 1: Active vs. Passive Status**
- Flipping property is classified as **active engagement in a trade or business**
- When utilizing an IRA for flipping, the tax-exempt entity competes with taxpaying businesses

**Rule 2: UBIT Tax Trigger**
- **UBIT Tax Rate:** Can be as high as **37%**
- **Trigger Condition:** IRA flips property on a **"regular or repeated" basis**
- **IRS Evaluation Criteria:** 
  - Number of flips
  - Level of involvement in activity

**Rule 3: IRS Interpretation**
- "Regular or repeated" is subject to interpretation—no clear threshold is defined
- IRS evaluates frequency and activity level to determine UBIT applicability

⚠️ **CRITICAL WARNING:** Consult with a professional tax advisor before any IRA real estate investment to understand UBIT implications.

## STRATEGIES & METHODS

### Strategy 1: The 1-Year Hold Method (UBIT Avoidance)

**Process:**
1. Purchase a discounted property
2. Renovate using IRA funds (standard approach)
3. **Hold property as rental for minimum 1 year**
4. After 1-year period, sell property and realize profit

**Tax Outcome:** Tax-free or tax-deferred gain (UBIT does NOT apply)

**Mechanism:** Property held as passive investment, then sold—does NOT trigger UBIT classification

**Use Case:** Suitable when flipping frequency may exceed IRS "regular or repeated" threshold

---

### Strategy 2: Private Money Lending Alternative

**Description:** 
> If you prefer an even more passive strategy to invest your IRA, consider being a private money lender to real estate investors.

**Characteristics:**
- Extremely passive role
- Can earn double-digit returns
- If structured correctly, maintains passive investment status
- Lender provides capital to other real estate investors

**Benefit:** Avoids active business engagement classification entirely
