## THREE METHODS TO STRUCTURE WHOLESALE DEALS

### Method 1: Buy and Re-Sell
- Wholesaler purchases property outright
- Wholesaler then re-sells to end buyer
- Requires transactional funding (see Section: Transactional Funding)

### Method 2: Assignment
- Wholesaler contracts the property
- Wholesaler assigns contract rights to end buyer
- Wholesaler never takes title
- Assignment fee collected at closing

### Method 3: Option Agreement
- Wholesaler secures option to purchase
- Maintains control without ownership
- Details referenced in Chapter 4

## TARGET MARKET ANALYSIS

### Three Target Buyer Categories

#### 1. FIX AND FLIP INVESTOR
- **Profile:** Purchases properties to renovate and resell quickly
- **Timeline:** Short-term hold (typically 6-12 months)
- **Motivation:** Profit from appreciation after repairs
- **Payment:** Typically cash or hard money
- **Reference:** Chapter 2

#### 2. BUY AND HOLD INVESTOR
- **Profile:** Purchases for long-term rental income
- **Timeline:** Long-term hold (5+ years)
- **Motivation:** Monthly cash flow from tenants
- **Payment:** Conventional financing or cash
- **Reference:** Chapter 2

#### 3. RETAIL BUYER
- **Profile:** Owner-occupant, primary residence buyer
- **Timeline:** Long-term hold (owner lives there)
- **Motivation:** Homeownership, personal use
- **Payment:** FHA, conventional, VA financing
- **Reference:** Chapter 2

### Market Selection Process
- Determine ideal target buyer for your market
- Analyze market trends
- Reference: Chapter 2 - "Determining Your Ideal Target Buyer and Market"
