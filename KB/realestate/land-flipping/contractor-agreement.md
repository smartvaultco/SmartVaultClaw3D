### 2. Bonus & Penalty Structure

**Template Language:**
- Early completion bonus: `$__________________ per day`
- Late completion penalty: `$__________________ per day` (withheld from final draw)
- Both tied to completion date in Item 10

**Strategy:** Use incentives to compress timeline for faster project turnover.

---

### 3. Scope of Work & Specifications

> The home is to be renovated in accordance with "Scope of Work" approved by the Client as outlined and attached in Addendum A to this agreement.

**Required Attachments:**
- Addendum A (detailed Scope of Work)

**Approval Workflow:**
1. Client selects finish materials (Section 4)
2. All selections documented before work begins

---

### 4. Change Order Process

**Critical Timeline Rule:**
- Changes should be addressed **at or before the start date**
- Changes after start date are "very burdensome to the Contractor"

**Change Order Requirements:**
- Must be submitted in writing
- Changes resulting in additional work require written agreement BEFORE performance
- Both parties must agree in advance

**Material Substitution Rights:**
> Contractor, therefore, reserves the right to substitute items of similar price and quality to those specified, with Client notification.

**Approval Flow for Minor Alterations:**
1. Contractor identifies field alteration or substitution needed
2. Submit to Client for approval
3. Proceed only after Client notification received

---

**Arbitration Framework (Not Litigation):**

> Any dispute between Contractor and Client about this agreement, including the interpretation of this agreement and adequacy of performance of this agreement, shall be resolved by arbitration before a single arbitrator who is mutually acceptable to Contractor and Buyer.

**Arbitrator Selection:**
- If no mutual agreement: President of Builder's Association of `_________________` appoints arbitrator
- Arbitrator must be licensed builder actively engaged in residential building

**Binding Nature:**

**Cost Allocation:**

**Strategy:** Arbitration is faster and cheaper than litigation; prefer this clause for contractor disputes.

## Critical Non-Negotiables & Rules

### For Contractor Selection

1. **Must be licensed** in the state where work is performed
2. **Must provide** current liability insurance certificates
3. **Must use licensed sub-contractors** where required by law
4. **Must provide** detailed draw invoices and lien waivers
5. **Must maintain** insurance throughout project duration

### For Project Management

1. **Changes before start date only** — post-start changes are "very burdensome"
2. **All alterations submitted in writing** — no verbal change approvals
3. **30-day notice required** before walk-thru inspection
4. **Written notice required** for any date modifications
5. **Final payment withheld** until punch list completed
6. **Certificate of Occupancy required** before completion declared

### For Client Protection

1. **Limited warranty only** — no merchantability or habitability warranties
2. **Warranty voids upon resale** — non-transferable
3. **Warranty limited to _______ months** — set notification deadline
4. **Client selects all finish materials** — contractor not liable for selection
5. **Client approves all substitutions** — contractor reserves right with notification
6. **Arbitration mandatory** — avoids costly litigation

## Warning Flags & Risk Mitigation

### Contractor Red Flags

⚠️ **Unlicensed contractors** — violates Section 8 (Construction Compliance)

⚠️ **Reluctance to carry insurance** — non-compliance with Section 10

⚠️ **No detailed Scope of Work provided** — cannot reference Addendum A

⚠️ **Unwilling to provide draw documentation** — creates payment disputes

⚠️ **Proposing oral modifications** — violates Section 17 (Entire Agreement)

### Client Protection Checkpoints

1. **Before signing:** Verify contractor license and insurance
2. **Before work starts:** Finalize Addendum A (Scope of Work)
3. **Before work starts:** Lock in all selections and finishes
4. **During project:** Request draws with proper documentation
5. **Before final payment:** Complete walk-thru inspection and punch list
6. **Post-completion:** Retain copy of all lien waivers and warranties
