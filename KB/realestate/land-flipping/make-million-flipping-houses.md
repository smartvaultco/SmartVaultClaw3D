## BUSINESS MODEL EVOLUTION & KEY INSIGHTS

### Wholesaling to Turnkey to Retail Flipping Progression

#### Phase 1: Wholesaling (2005)
- Found distressed properties at discount
- Sold to out-of-state/foreign buy-and-hold investors
- Initial profit: $8,000/deal average (example from mentor wholesaler)
- **Lesson learned**: Market decline (2006 Michigan crash) created opportunity with non-local investors

#### Phase 2: Value-Add Turnkey (2006–2007)
- **Obstacle identified**: Out-of-state investors couldn't manage rehabs remotely; bottleneck halted repeat purchases
- **Solution**: Began managing rehabs for investors (to unblock sales pipeline)
- **Value escalation**: Added property management + tenant placement
- **Result**: Sold completely renovated, leased, managed properties ("done for you")
- **Volume**: ~70 properties in 2007 to national/international investors

#### Phase 3: Suburban Retail Flip (Post-2007)
- **Target shift**: End-buyer homeowners in nice suburban areas (vs. investor rental properties)
- **Profit increase**: $25,000+ net per deal
- **Operational question driving optimization**: *"How can I do this better, faster and get someone else to do the work for me while making more money?"*
- **System result**: Perfected, duplicable framework used across multiple US markets

### Key Strategic Principles

> **The more value you provide, the more they will continue to buy.**

- Volume is driven by removing bottlenecks (rehab management)
- Turnkey positioning justifies premium pricing
- Systemization allows geographic scalability
