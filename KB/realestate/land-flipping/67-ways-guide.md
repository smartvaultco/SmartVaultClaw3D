## CORE CONCEPT: MOTIVATED SELLER DEFINITION

> "A motivated seller is someone who is willing to trade equity for peace of mind. In other words, they will sell you their unwanted property at a discount to alleviate the stress that property is causing them."

---

## MARKETING METHODOLOGY: PUSH vs. PULL

### Push Marketing (Proactive Outreach)
- Direct contact to prospects
- Methods: Calling, texting, emailing, ringless voicemail (RVM), door knocking
- Bot Action: Initiate contact with leads

### Pull Marketing (Attraction-Based)
- Place information in front of prospects
- Prospects reach out to you
- Methods: Direct mail, bandit signs, advertisements
- Bot Action: Establish presence on relevant channels

**Strategic Approach:**
> "With all of the motivated seller lead types I'm going to share, think about employing both push and pull techniques. At the end of the day, your goal is to get in front of a motivated seller lead and make an offer to buy their distressed property."

## 67 WAYS TO FIND MOTIVATED SELLERS

### CATEGORY 1: LEGAL/COURT RECORDS

#### 1. Eviction Notices from Court
- **Source:** Local court handling tenant evictions
- **Action:** Contact court for eviction notice lists
- **Rationale:** Tired landlords motivated to exit property

#### 6. Tax Delinquent Homeowners
- **Source:** County assessor's office
- **Action:** Request tax delinquent list
- **Targeting:** Start with delinquents 1+ years behind

#### 29. Pre Pre-Foreclosure
- **Definition:** Late on payments, bank hasn't started foreclosure
- **Urgency:** High—early intervention opportunity

#### 30. Pre-Foreclosure
- **Definition:** Bank has initiated foreclosure process
- **Resource:** Video tutorial available
- **Urgency:** Critical—time-sensitive

#### 35. Bankruptcy/Financial Burden Owners
- **Motivation:** Need to sell to meet financial obligations

#### 36. Bankruptcy Court Listings
- **Source:** Official bankruptcy court records
- **Action:** Monitor and contact filers

---

### CATEGORY 2: TAX & PROPERTY ASSESSMENTS

#### 3. Landlords with Back Taxes on Multiple Properties
- **Indicator:** Financial problem
- **Motivation:** May sell property quickly to pay back taxes
- **Action:** Target multi-property owners with tax delinquency

#### 4. Property Assessment Increases
- **Source:** County assessor's office
- **Action:** Identify largest assessment increases
- **Rationale:** Higher property taxes → owners may not afford home

### CATEGORY 3: PERSONAL LIFE CHANGES

#### 14. People Getting Divorced
- **Status:** May be motivated to sell home
- **Contact Method:** Direct outreach or through attorney referral

#### 15. Divorce Attorneys
- **Relationship:** Can refer divorce clients
- **Method:** Build referral relationship

#### 26. Empty Nesters
- **Definition:** Owners with equity, lived 30+ years in home
- **Motivation:** Ready to downsize
- **Video Resource:** https://youtu.be/usIfNTcnvtU
- **Note:** Identified as next biggest wave of motivated sellers

#### 32. Work Transfer Relocations
- **Profile:** Transferred for employment
- **Motivation:** Often motivated to sell

#### 33. Medical/Health Problems
- **Motivation:** Downsize or sell to pay medical expenses

---

### CATEGORY 4: LANDLORD/INVESTOR FATIGUE

#### 2. Out-of-State Landlords
- **Profile:** Own rental in different state from residence
- **Trigger:** Job relocation—kept primary residence as rental
- **Pain Point:** Difficulty managing long-distance rental
- **Motivation:** Motivated to sell quickly

#### 31. Double Payment Owners
- **Definition:** Own 2 homes, owe on both
- **Motivation:** Financial burden → willing to sell

#### 34. Vacant Homes
- **Rationale:** Owner paying expenses with no occupancy
- **Motivation:** Carrying costs create urgency

#### 43. Section 8 Approved Property Owners
- **Profile:** Burned out or tired landlord
- **Opportunity:** Section 8 creates management burden

## PROPERTY-SPECIFIC TARGETING CRITERIA

### Financial Distress Indicators
- Back taxes on multiple properties
- Property assessment increases above area average
- Tax delinquency (1+ years)
- Late mortgage payments
- Bankruptcy filing

### Motivation Triggers
- Eviction proceedings
- Job relocation/transfer
- Medical/health issues
- Divorce proceedings
- Death/inheritance
- Business failure
- Job loss/unemployment
- Violent crime arrest

### Investor Burn-Out Signals
- Out-of-state ownership
- Multiple properties with financial issues
- Section 8 properties
- Long-term ownership (30+ years)
- Vacant properties
