## PRIVATE MONEY LENDING — 6-STEP PROCESS

### Step 1: Present the Facts
**Information to Present to Private Lender:**
- Breakdown of numbers (purchase price, rehab costs, ARV, turn-around time, etc.)
- Comparable sales (comps) to justify ARV
- As-is before pictures
- Estimated closing date

**Goal:** The more information confirming it's a good deal, the more likely the lender will fund it.

---

### Step 2: Draft Paperwork
**Two Main Documents Required:**
1. Promissory note
2. Mortgage or deed of trust (varies by state)

**Process:**
- Review and verify all terms are satisfactory
- Sign and notarize originals
- Overnight original documents to private investor

---

### Step 3: Closing on the Purchase
**Private Investor Receives:**
- Copy of closing/settlement statement
- Wiring instructions
- Insurance certificate

**Funding Method:**
- Private investor wires funds directly to title/closing company (NOT to you)
- Title/closing company records mortgage or deed of trust with county
- Recorded copy sent to private investor

---

### Step 4: Renovations — Draw Process
**Reimbursement Structure:**
Rehab funded in draws for completed work only.

**Submission Requirements for Each Draw:**
- Itemized breakdown of completed work
- Pictures of completed work
- Amount of draw requested

**Lender Response:**
- Private investor sends draw (as reimbursement or payment to contractors)

---

### Step 5: Monthly Payments (Debt Investors Only)
**For Non-Equity Investors:**
- Monthly interest-only payments to private investor
- Amount based on pro-rated balance
- Balance = Purchase price + cumulative draws received

---

### Step 6: Closing on the Sale
**Process:**
1. Renovations complete, retail buyer secured, closing date scheduled
2. Private investor provides payoff letter from title/closing agency (principal + pro-rated interest)
3. Title/closing company sends payoff proceeds directly to private investor at closing
4. Private investor becomes whole, lien released

## CREATIVE FINANCING STRATEGIES

### Core Philosophy
> Cash = pay less.
> Terms = pay more.

**Key Principle:**
Using creative financing, you don't have to factor in the expensive cost of cash. Therefore, you can pay the seller more, which allows you to do more deals—deals that cash-only investors will pass on.

**Mutual Benefit:**
- **Seller benefit:** If willing to wait, earns more money than selling for cash outright
- **Buyer benefit:** Can pay more to acquire same property without needing cash upfront

---

## TWO-STEP OFFER PROCESS FOR CREATIVE FINANCING

### Step 1: Make a Cash Offer
- Present low all-cash offer
- Position as fastest and most convenient exit for seller
- Frame logic: "All-cash" + "close quickly" = property permanently out of seller's life = worth lower sale price

### Step 2: Counter with Creative Financing
**If seller counters at higher price OR rejects all-cash offer:**
- Counter back with creative financing solution
- Offer to pay MORE if seller accepts creative financing terms

**Psychological Framing for Seller:**

> "If you want to be rid of this property as fast as possible and you want your money now…here's my all-cash offer but if you're willing to wait to get paid and are willing to be flexible on terms, then I can pay you more for it."

**Distilled Choice for Seller:**
> Less money now or more money later…
> Which is more important?

## CREATIVE FINANCING METHOD #1: SELLER FINANCING

### Definition
Also called "owner financing" or "owner carry-back."

**Structure:**
- Seller agrees to sell property with little to no money down
- Buyer assumes responsibility for property ownership
- Seller carries balance owed as seller-finance loan
- Seller becomes the lender on their own property

**Example Structure:**
- Property sale price: $100,000
- Rather than: Cash payment OR 3rd party financing (bank, private lender, hard money)
- Seller finances the balance and holds promissory note + mortgage/deed of trust

## KEY FINANCIAL FORMULAS & CALCULATIONS

### Pro-Rated Balance for Interest-Only Payments
**Formula:**
Interest Payment = (Purchase Price + Cumulative Draws) × Interest Rate × (Days in Period / 365)

**Application:**
Monthly interest-only payments based on pro-rated balance as draws are added during renovation phase.

## DOCUMENT RECORDING PROCESS

**Title Company Responsibilities:**
1. Receive wire from private investor
2. Record mortgage or deed of trust with county
3. Send recorded copy to private investor (proof of lien)
4. At sale closing, process payoff and distribute to lender

**Why This Matters:** Protects lender position and ensures investor can't access funds without satisfying loan obligations.
