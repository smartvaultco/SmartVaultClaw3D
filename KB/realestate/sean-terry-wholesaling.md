## THE CORE CONCEPT

Virtual wholesaling = find a motivated seller, lock the property under contract at a discount, assign the contract to a cash buyer for a fee. You never own the property. You never need cash or credit. You can do it from anywhere.

**Assignment fee target:** $5,000 – $25,000 per deal
**Capital required:** $0 (just tools + marketing cost)
**Time to first deal:** 30-90 days working the system consistently

---

## THE MOTIVATED SELLER — WHO THEY ARE

A motivated seller is someone who NEEDS to sell, not just WANTS to sell.

**The Big 5 Motivated Seller Types:**
1. **Tax delinquent** — behind on property taxes, county can seize the home
2. **Pre-foreclosure** — missed mortgage payments, foreclosure notice filed (NOD)
3. **Probate** — owner died, heirs want to liquidate the property quickly
4. **Absentee owners** — own property but don't live there (landlords with problem tenants, inherited out-of-state)
5. **Code violations** — city has cited them for violations, fines accumulating

**What makes them say yes to a low offer:**
- Divorce (need it done fast, can't coordinate with ex)
- Job loss / financial hardship
- Property is in bad condition — they can't afford to fix it
- Inherited property they don't want to manage from out of state
- Tired landlord — fed up with tenants, just wants out
- Facing foreclosure — they'll take less to avoid ruining their credit

## LIST PULLING — WHERE TO FIND SELLERS

**Primary Tool: PropStream** (propstream.com)
- Pull by zip code, county, or state
- Filter: tax delinquent + absentee owner = highest motivation combo
- Export 500-1,000 leads at a time

**BatchLeads** (batchleads.io)
- Skip tracing built in — finds phone numbers + emails
- SMS campaigns directly from platform
- Pull pre-foreclosure and driving for dollars lists

**County Records (free)**
- Tax delinquent lists: contact county tax assessor office
- Pre-foreclosure: PACER system or county courthouse records
- Probate: county probate court filings (search by recent death + real property)

**List Priority Order (highest to lowest conversion):**
1. Tax delinquent + absentee owner (2 filters = double motivation)
2. Pre-foreclosure (30-90 days behind — window before they lose it)
3. Probate (heirs want cash, not a property)
4. Absentee owner only (landlord fatigue)
5. Code violations

## OUTREACH — THE 3-CHANNEL ATTACK

### Channel 1: Cold Calling (Highest Conversion)
**Use:** Bland.ai (AI voice) for first touch, then human (or Closer Bot) for qualified leads

**Sean Terry's Cold Call Script:**

Opening:
> "Hi, I'm trying to reach [NAME]. My name is [NAME], I'm a real estate investor. I saw your property at [ADDRESS] and I wanted to reach out to see if you'd have any interest in selling. Is that something you'd be open to?"

If yes / maybe:
> "Great. I work with a group of investors and we buy properties as-is, any condition, very fast closings, all cash. Can I ask — what would you need to get out of the property to make it worth your while?"

**Let them talk.** Don't fill silence. Their number is the opening. Don't anchor first.

If asking why selling:
> "We find a lot of times people reach out to us because there's some kind of situation — whether it's the property needing work, some life event, or just wanting to move on. Is there a particular reason you'd consider selling?"

**Objection: "I'm not interested"**
> "No problem at all. Out of curiosity, is it because you feel like the price wouldn't be right, or is it just not the right time?"

**Objection: "I want full market value"**
> "I totally understand. We wouldn't be able to do full market value because we're buying as-is and cash — but if the condition or situation makes a quick sale worth it to you, we might still be a fit. What would you need?"

**Objection: "I have a realtor"**
> "Perfect, that works. A lot of the sellers we work with actually have realtors. If for some reason that doesn't work out or you want a guaranteed cash offer before listing, keep our number. Would that be okay?"

## THE OFFER FORMULA

**ARV** = After Repair Value (what it's worth fixed up — pull from Zillow/MLS comps)
**Repairs** = estimate (use $15-25/sqft for light rehab, $35-50/sqft for full gut)
**MAO** = Maximum Allowable Offer

```
MAO = (ARV × 0.70) − Repairs − Wholesale Fee
```

Example:
- ARV: $200,000
- Repairs: $30,000
- Wholesale fee: $10,000
- MAO = ($200K × 0.70) − $30K − $10K = $100,000

Never go above MAO. If seller won't accept — walk away.

**Getting the ARV fast:**
- Zillow "Recently Sold" — filter same sqft, beds, baths, within 1 mile, last 90 days
- PropStream comps — more accurate for investor purposes
- Call a local realtor — "I'm an investor, can you pull comps for me?" Most will, for free, hoping to list future deals

## LOCKING UP THE CONTRACT

**Use:** Simple 1-2 page purchase agreement (wholesaling contract)

Key clauses to include:
- "And/Or Assigns" after buyer name (this is what gives you the right to assign)
- Inspection period: 7-10 days (gives you time to find a buyer before closing)
- Closing date: 21-30 days
- Earnest money: $100-500 (shows you're serious but keeps risk low)

**Send via DocuSign** — fully electronic, legal in all 50 states

## FINDING CASH BUYERS — THE BUYER'S LIST

You sell the contract to a cash buyer (rehabber or landlord). They close, you get paid.

**How to build the cash buyer list:**
1. Craigslist — post "Wholesale deal — cash buyers needed in [CITY]"
2. Facebook Groups — search "[CITY] real estate investors" — post deals
3. BiggerPockets — direct message investors in your target market
4. Google — "[CITY] we buy houses" — call those companies, they're cash buyers
5. County records — search recent cash sales (no mortgage = cash buyer) — cold call them
6. REI clubs — local real estate investor associations (meetup.com/REI)

**What cash buyers want:**
- 70% of ARV or less (all-in)
- As-is condition — no repairs before purchase
- Fast close (14-21 days)
- Clear title

**When you have a deal under contract:**
> "I have a 3/2 at [ADDRESS] under contract at $X. ARV is approximately $Y, needs about $Z in repairs. Buyers in my list get first look. Reply if interested — closes in 21 days."

Broadcast to entire buyer list. First one to sign the assignment agreement wins.

---

## THE ASSIGNMENT OF CONTRACT

When you find a cash buyer:
- Sign an Assignment of Contract (1-page document)
- States: Buyer pays you $[WHOLESALE FEE] to take over the original purchase agreement
- Buyer takes the contract — you get your check at closing
- Title company handles the close — you don't need to be there

**Assignment fee collected:** At closing, wired to your LLC bank account

## FOLLOW-UP — THE MONEY IS IN THE FOLLOW-UP

Most sellers say no the first time. 80% of deals come from follow-up.

**Follow-up sequence (in GHL):**
- Day 1: Initial contact
- Day 3: Follow-up call/text
- Day 7: Check-in
- Day 14: "Any change in your situation?"
- Day 30: "Still thinking about selling?"
- Day 60: "Market update — just closed a deal nearby, curious if you're still open"
- Day 90: "Last check-in — if you ever want an offer, I'm here"
- Monthly after: Drip forever — sellers often take 6-18 months to decide

**GHL automation handles this entire sequence.** Real Estate Bot triggers it after every contact.
