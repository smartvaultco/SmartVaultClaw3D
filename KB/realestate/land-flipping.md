## Knowledge Base for Real Estate Bot

## WHY LAND FLIPPING

- No tenants, no toilets, no termites
- No repairs — raw land needs nothing
- Sellers are often heirs who never wanted it — motivated to sell cheap
- Buy at 15-25 cents on dollar → sell at 80-100% of market value
- Profit margins: 200-500% per deal
- Can be done 100% virtually — no boots on the ground needed
- Average deal: $3,000-$30,000 profit

## FIND LAND TO BUY

**Step 1 — Pick a county**

Target: Rural counties with:
- Low median land prices ($5K-$50K per acre)
- High tax delinquency (means owners don't value the land)
- Active land listings on Zillow/LandWatch (means buyers exist)
- Near growing metro areas or desirable regions

**Good starting states:** Texas, Florida, Arizona, New Mexico, Tennessee, North Carolina, Missouri

**Step 2 — Pull the list**

Go to county tax assessor website → download tax delinquent list filtered for:
- Land/vacant only (no structures)
- Taxes delinquent 1+ years
- Parcel size: 1-100 acres (sweet spot)
- No liens over $5,000

Many counties will email you the list for free. Some charge $50-200.

**Step 3 — Skip trace**
Use BatchLeads or PropStream to find owner names + mailing addresses.
Many land owners have moved — skip trace to find current address.

## SEND BLIND OFFERS BY MAIL

**The genius of Jack Bosch's method:** Send an actual offer letter before they even know you're interested. Most land owners are shocked anyone wants their forgotten parcel.

**Pricing your blind offer:**
- Pull recent sold comps: Zillow, Land.com, LandWatch — filter same county, similar acreage
- Offer 15-25% of median comp price
- Example: 10 acres comps sell for $20,000 → offer $3,000-$5,000

**The blind offer letter:**
```
Dear [OWNER NAME],

My name is [NAME] and I'm a real estate investor looking to purchase vacant land
in [COUNTY], [STATE].

I'd like to make you an offer to purchase your property at:
[LEGAL DESCRIPTION / PARCEL NUMBER]
[ADDRESS IF AVAILABLE]

My offer is $[AMOUNT], cash, paid directly to you at closing.
This is an as-is, all-cash offer with a quick 30-day close.
No realtor fees. No commissions. Just a fast, simple transaction.

If you're interested, please sign and return the enclosed acceptance form
in the postage-paid envelope, or call/text me at [NUMBER].

If this offer doesn't work for you, no worries at all. I appreciate your time.

Sincerely,
[NAME]
```

**Mail via LetterStream.** Send 300-500 letters per campaign. Expect 2-5% response rate.

## DUE DILIGENCE — BEFORE YOU SIGN

When a seller accepts, verify before closing:

1. **Access** — Does the parcel have road access? (Landlocked land = nearly worthless)
   - Check county GIS map — is there a road touching the parcel?
   - If no road access, negotiate for an easement or walk away

2. **Zoning** — What can you do with it?
   - Call county planning/zoning office: "What is this parcel zoned for and what can be built on it?"
   - Agricultural, residential, commercial each have different values

3. **Flood zone** — Is it in a FEMA flood zone?
   - Go to msc.fema.gov → enter address → check flood zone designation
   - Zone X = no flood risk (good). Zone AE = flood zone (bad — hard to sell)

4. **Liens / Back taxes** — Is it clean?
   - Title search at county courthouse or order from title company ($100-200)
   - Back taxes you'll need to pay at closing — factor into offer

5. **HOA / Covenants** — Any restrictions on use?
   - Search county records for deed restrictions or HOA filings

**Quick due diligence: 30 minutes using free county GIS maps + FEMA flood map tool**

## CLOSING THE PURCHASE

**Two options:**

**Option A: Use a title company** (safest — ~$300-500 fee)
- Submit to local title company in the county
- They run title search, handle deed transfer, file with county
- You wire funds, they send you the deed
- Best for deals over $5,000

**Option B: Direct closing** (cheaper — use for deals under $3,000)
- Use a simple deed template for that state (warranty deed or quitclaim deed)
- File with county recorder yourself ($15-30 filing fee)
- Only do this if you have done the title search yourself and it's completely clean

## SELLING THE LAND

List on all platforms simultaneously. Land sells slower than houses — cast a wide net.

**Listing Platforms:**
1. **Zillow** — free, massive traffic, include all photos + description
2. **LandWatch.com** — land-specific, serious buyers
3. **Land.com** — large land marketplace
4. **LandFlip.com** — investor-focused buyers
5. **Facebook Marketplace** — fast + local buyers
6. **Craigslist** — free, still works for rural land
7. **Facebook Groups** — search "[STATE] land for sale" groups

**Pricing to sell:**
- Price at 80-90% of comparable retail sales
- Slightly under market = faster sale
- Include seller financing option (see below) to attract more buyers

**Listing description formula:**
```
[ACREAGE] Acres in [COUNTY], [STATE] — [BEST FEATURE]

[2-3 sentences about the land's best features: views, timber, water, proximity to town]

Property Details:
- Size: X acres
- Zoning: [ZONING]
- Access: [Road frontage / Easement]
- Utilities: [Available / None — off-grid potential]
- Taxes: $X/year
- Coordinates: [GPS coordinates for easy navigation]
- APN: [Parcel number]

Cash price: $[PRICE]
Owner financing available: $[DOWN] down, $[PAYMENT]/month

Call/text [NUMBER] or reply to this listing.
```

## SELLER FINANCING — THE MULTIPLIER

Instead of selling for a lump sum, offer monthly payments. This:
- Attracts 3-5x more buyers (most people don't have $15K cash but can do $200/month)
- Gets you a higher total sale price
- Creates monthly passive income

**Example:**
- You bought: $2,000
- Cash price: $12,000
- OR: $500 down + $199/month for 60 months = $12,440 total (more money + monthly income)

**Seller financing note terms:**
- Interest rate: 8-12% (you set the rate — no bank involved)
- Term: 36-84 months
- Down payment: 10-20% of purchase price
- If they stop paying → you get the land back (foreclose) and sell again

**Tools for managing notes:**
- Paynearme or ACH payment processing
- Track in simple spreadsheet or PropStream
