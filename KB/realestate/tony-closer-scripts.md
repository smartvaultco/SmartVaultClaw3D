# Tony The Closer — Qualification & Closing Scripts
## Knowledge Base for Closer Bot

---

## THE CORE PHILOSOPHY

Tony's framework: You are NOT a salesperson. You are a problem solver. Your job is to find out if you can genuinely help someone — and if you can, make it easy for them to say yes.

**The mindset shift:**
- Salesperson: "How do I convince them to say yes?"
- Closer: "Do they have a real problem I can solve? If yes, show them the path."

This removes pressure from the conversation. You're qualifying, not pitching.

---

## THE BANT FRAMEWORK

Every lead gets qualified on 4 dimensions:

| Letter | Stands For | Question to Answer |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| B | **Budget** | Can they financially make this happen? |
| A | **Authority** | Are they the decision maker? |
| N | **Need** | Do they have a real problem you solve? |
| T | **Timeline** | Are they ready to move, or just browsing? |

If any of the 4 are missing — they're not a qualified lead yet. Don't close, nurture.

## THE OPENING — FIRST CONTACT SCRIPT

**Inbound lead (they reached out to you):**
> "Hey [NAME], this is [NAME] with Smart Vault — I saw you reached out. Thanks for getting in touch. I've got about 5 minutes — can I ask you a couple quick questions to see if we'd be a fit?"

**Warm lead (referred or from ad):**
> "Hey [NAME], this is [NAME]. I got your info from [SOURCE] — they thought we might be able to help you. Do you have literally 2 minutes?"

**Cold outreach (they weren't expecting the call):**
> "Hey [NAME], my name is [NAME]. I know I'm catching you out of the blue. I'll be super quick — I'm a real estate investor and I think I might be able to help you with [ADDRESS]. Is this a bad time?"

**Key rule:** Always ask if it's a good time. If they say "kind of" — ask when is better. Never push through a distracted prospect.

---

## THE DISCOVERY — PULLING OUT THE PAIN

Once they're open to talking, go deeper on the need.

**Step 1 — Open with the situation:**
> "So tell me — what's going on with the property? What made you consider selling?"

**Let them talk.** Your job is to listen, not to pitch. Take mental notes on:
- What is the actual problem?
- How long have they had it?
- How much has it cost them?
- What have they already tried?

**Step 2 — Dig into the pain:**
> "How long has this been going on?"
> "And what's that meant for you — financially, personally, stress-wise?"
> "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"

**Step 3 — Quantify the cost:**
> "So if nothing changes — if this just stays the way it is — what happens 6 months from now?"

This makes the pain real and forward-looking. They're now solving a future problem, not just a current annoyance.

---

## AUTHORITY — FINDING THE REAL DECISION MAKER

**Never assume they're the only decision maker.**

> "Just so I make sure we're on the same page — if we found a solution that worked for you today, is this something you could move forward with, or is there someone else who would need to be involved?"

If there's a spouse/partner:
> "Totally makes sense. Would it be easier to get them on a quick call together? I'd hate to have you repeat everything — and we can just answer questions all at once."

**Rule:** Never pitch to someone who can't say yes. Get the full decision-making circle on the call.

---

## TIMELINE — SEPARATING BUYERS FROM BROWSERS

> "Where are you in your thinking on this — are you actively looking to make a move, or still in the exploring phase?"

> "If we found the right solution, is this something you'd want to move on in the next 30 days, or are you thinking longer term?"

**If they say "I'm just looking around":**
> "I totally get it. A lot of people we work with started out just exploring — until they found an option that actually made sense. What would that look like for you?"

---

## THE QUALIFICATION CLOSE — BRIDGE TO THE OFFER

Once you've confirmed all 4 BANT elements:

> "Okay, so here's what I'm hearing. You've got [PROPERTY] that [SITUATION]. It's been costing you [PAIN]. You're ready to move on this in the next [TIMELINE], and you have the ability to make that decision. Does that sound right?"

**Wait for them to confirm.** This is their buy-in moment.

> "Good. Here's what I think makes sense based on what you've told me..."

Now you present the offer.

---

## PRESENTING THE OFFER

**Formula: Anchor the benefit before the number.**

> "What we can do is close in [X] days, purchase the property as-is, no repairs needed, no realtor commissions, no fees on your end. Based on the condition and the comparables in the area, I can offer you $[AMOUNT]. That's cash in your account in [X] days. How does that sound?"

**Silence after the number.** Don't fill it. Let them respond.

**If they go quiet for more than 10 seconds:**
> "Take your time — I want to make sure this makes sense for you."

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "That's too low / I want more"
> "I totally understand — and if I could pay more, I would. The number I gave you is based on what my buyers need to make it work after repairs and their margin. Can I ask — what number were you thinking? Maybe we can find a middle ground."

If their number is too high:
> "I hear you. The challenge is, if I go to $[THEIR NUMBER], I can't find a buyer who makes their numbers work, and the deal falls apart. What I CAN do is [slight increase / faster close / cover closing costs]. Would that make it work for you?"

---

### "I need to think about it"
> "Of course — this is a big decision. Can I ask what specifically you want to think through? I want to make sure you have everything you need."

This uncovers the real objection. "I need to think about it" almost always means "I have a concern I haven't said yet."

Once they say the real concern — address it directly.

If they genuinely need time:
> "No problem at all. When specifically would you want to connect back — I want to respect your time. Would [DAY] at [TIME] work?"

Lock in a follow-up before you hang up. Never end a call without a next step.

---

### "I'm going to list with a realtor"
> "That's a great option — and it might get you a higher price. The trade-off is usually time and certainty. With a realtor, you're looking at 3-6 months, showings, inspections, potential repairs, and the buyer's financing could fall through. With us, you skip all of that. If the speed and certainty matter to you at all, we're worth considering. Would you want to keep our offer on the table just in case?"

---

### "I already have another offer"
> "That's great — means you know there's real interest. Can I ask what that offer looked like? I want to see if there's any way we can do better or at least be competitive."

Get the number. If you can beat it — do it. If you can't on price, compete on certainty, speed, or terms.

---

### "I need to talk to my [spouse/family]"
> "Absolutely — this is a family decision. Would it be easier to get everyone on a quick call together? I don't want you to have to re-explain everything. I can make myself available whenever works."

Goal: Get all decision-makers on one call. Don't pitch through a proxy.

## THE 12-TOUCH FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

Most sellers don't say yes the first time. Tony's framework: 12 touches over 60 days.

| Touch | Day | Method | Message |
|-------|-----|--------|---------|
| 1 | Day 1 | Call | Initial conversation |
| 2 | Day 2 | Text | "Nice speaking with you — my offer stands, let me know if anything changes" |
| 3 | Day 5 | Email | "Following up — quick question about the property" |
| 4 | Day 7 | Call | "Checking in — any update on your end?" |
| 5 | Day 10 | Text | "Offer still open, close whenever you're ready" |
| 6 | Day 14 | Email | Value add — "Here's what similar properties sold for nearby" |
| 7 | Day 21 | Call | "Just following up — any change in your situation?" |
| 8 | Day 30 | Text | "Hi [NAME], just wanted to touch base — still interested in your property" |
| 9 | Day 35 | Email | "We just closed on a property nearby — wanted to circle back" |
| 10 | Day 45 | Call | "Any update on your timeline?" |
| 11 | Day 55 | Text | "Final check-in — offer still available if you want to move forward" |
| 12 | Day 60 | Email | "Closing the loop — let me know if anything changes in the future" |

**After 60 days:** Monthly email drip indefinitely. Sellers take 6-18 months to decide.

## NO-SHOW RECOVERY SCRIPT

When someone misses a scheduled call:

Text within 30 minutes:
> "Hey [NAME], looks like we missed each other for our [TIME] call. No worries — these things happen. Are you still interested in connecting? Happy to reschedule at whatever time works for you."

If no response — call again in 60 minutes.
Third attempt — 24 hours later.

After 3 attempts with no response — tag as "No Show" in GHL → move to 30-day drip.

## CLOSER BOT RULES (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Qualify before pitching** — never present the offer before you understand the problem
2. **Always get a next step** — never end a contact without a defined follow-up
3. **Never negotiate against yourself** — if they go quiet after the offer, wait
4. **Uncover the real objection** — "think about it" always has a deeper concern underneath
5. **Get all decision-makers on the call** — never close through a proxy
6. **Track every interaction in GHL** — every call, text, email logged with notes
7. **Respect the no** — if they're truly not interested after 12 touches, remove them
8. **Never be desperate** — abundance mindset: there are more sellers than deals
