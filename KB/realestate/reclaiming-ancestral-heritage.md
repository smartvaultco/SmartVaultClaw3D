## Step-by-Step Genealogical Research Guide

### Phase 1: Start With Yourself
1. Gather YOUR birth records, marriage records, army/war registration records
2. Move backwards: collect parents' birth records, death records, marriage certificates
3. Interview living elders — RECORD conversations (video/audio). Write down exact dates, names (first, last, maiden, before/after marriage)

### Phase 2: Local & State Research
4. Vital Statistics Buildings: request grandparents' generation birth/death records (expect runaround)
5. County Records: gather from specific counties where ancestors were born, married, died
6. Public Libraries: call ahead to confirm direct computer access to National Archives (deeper than ancestry.com)
7. Online: search "folk.com" (likely Fold3) for specific historical records

### Phase 3: Federal Archives & Specific Documents
8. National Archives: order ORIGINAL documents (copies can be altered to hide true history)
9. Land & Treasury Deeds: search registration deeds and State Treasury deeds for properties seized for taxes
10. Census & Classification Rolls: check microfilm for racial/social classifications. Check ALL rolls (Freedman's etc.), not just "Indian rolls"
11. Passenger & Vessel Indexes: look up "Index to passenger list of vessels arriving at..." specific ports (Charleston SC, Hampton VA). If ancestors' names NOT on slave ship manifests = confirms they were already in America
