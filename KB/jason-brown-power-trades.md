## 1. Specific Trading Strategies

### Call Options
- Bullish strategy giving the right to control a stock at a specified price (strike price) for a certain amount of time
- Provides massive leverage — invest in high-priced stocks for a fraction of the cost

### Deep In-The-Money Calls
- For low-priced stocks (under $50), buy deep in-the-money call options (typically two strike prices in the money)
- Low-priced stocks move slowly, so this ensures your option captures dollar-for-dollar movement

### Put Options
- Bearish strategy giving the right to force someone to buy your stock at a specific price
- Profit from a falling market AND acts as insurance to protect your existing portfolio

### Iron Condors
- Net credit trade where you collect premium upfront
- Risk a defined amount (e.g., $5,000 to $10,000) and take profit early once you see it
- NEVER "ride it out to the sunset" — take profits when they appear

### Rental Income Strategy
- Use options (like covered calls) to rent out stocks you already own in retirement or brokerage accounts
- Generates monthly income from existing holdings

### Paid for Waiting Strategy
- Options strategy to generate yield on a stock that is simply moving sideways

### Swing Trading (30 Days or Less)
- Primary approach: take positions with the goal of making $500 to $1,000 in 30 days or less
- Avoid day trading — that's just trading time for dollars

### Shorting Stock
- Selling stock you don't own — carries UNLIMITED risk if the stock gaps up
- Only use about 5% of the time; buying put options is a much safer alternative

## 2. Risk Management Rules

- **Options vs. Stocks:** Buying options is mathematically less risky than buying stock because risk is strictly capped at the price paid for the contract. Stock ownership carries the risk of the company going to zero.
- **Earnings Protection:** NEVER hold a naked stock position through an earnings announcement without buying put options as insurance. Stocks can gap down significantly overnight, bypassing stop-loss orders.
- **Cut Losses Quickly:** You can do all the research correctly and still lose. Keep losses small, cut them quickly. Trading is a numbers game — be right big and wrong small.
- **No Penny Stocks:** Do not buy cheap stocks trading under $5. These are "casino stocks" heading toward bankruptcy. Use options to control high-quality, high-priced companies instead.

## 4. Stock Picking Criteria

### The Top-Down Approach
1. Analyze the overall market (like the S&P 500)
2. Drill down to see which specific sectors are performing well (e.g., Tech ETF XLK)
3. Pick out the best individual stocks within the winning sector

### Winner's Circle vs. Penalty Box
- Only buy stocks in the "Winner's Circle" (long-term uptrend)
- Completely avoid stocks in the "Penalty Box" (long-term downtrend)

### Personal Consumption Habits
- Look at products you rave about and use daily (Apple, Ulta Beauty, Costco) — buy their stock
- Look for companies with terrible customer service — bet against them via put options

### The 4 Rules for Spotting Major Trends
1. A revolutionary new product or category (e.g., the personal computer)
2. A revolutionary enhancement to an existing product with a clear winner and loser (e.g., iPhone vs. Blackberry)
3. A revolutionary new process or delivery system (e.g., Netflix streaming, cloud storage)
4. Changes in law that unlock new billion-dollar industries (e.g., legalized marijuana)

## 5. Technical Analysis Methods

### Custom Moving Averages
- 10-day, 20-day, and 50-day Exponential Moving Averages
- 200-day Simple Moving Average (colored red for visibility)

### Indicators
- **Bollinger Bands:** Configured to 18-length and 2 standard deviations to spot breakouts
- **MACD:** Gauge momentum
- **Stochastics:** Overbought/oversold levels

### Support, Resistance, and Channeling
- Resistance lines: where stock consistently hits a ceiling (SELL)
- Support lines: where stock consistently bounces (BUY)
- Stock bouncing between both = "channel"

### Volume and Candlesticks
- Multiple days of upward price with declining volume = rally running out of steam
- "Doji" candlestick (very small body) at the top of a run = market indecision, likely rollover

## 6. Key Mindset Principles

- **Treat Trading Like a College Degree:** Commit 3-5 years to learning. Losses are "tuition." Society accepts 4 years and $50K debt for a degree but quits trading after 6 months.
- **Embrace the "No" (Dating Analogy):** A loss is like getting a "no" when asking for a date. Don't let ego stop you from trying again. Let the law of large numbers work.
- **Money is Just Energy:** Remove emotional attachment to money. Keep living expenses low, create disposable income that replenishes monthly.
- **Don't Be Greedy:** Amateurs expect every stock to go to the moon. Professionals execute their plan, take target profit, and exit. (e.g., $94K in 40 minutes on a breakdown — then OUT)
- **Set Micro-Goals:** Define your ultimate "why" with a specific vision board. Start with micro-goals (make $50 to take Saturday off work) to prove the concept.

## 8. Advanced Strategies (From Updated Sources)

### The Strangle (Direction Neutral)
- Buy OTM call + OTM put around current price (often around earnings)
- Example: Intel at $49 → buy $50 call + $48 put for $2.60 combined → 53% return when Intel dropped $5

### Bull Call Spreads (Debit Spreads)
- Buy a call + sell a higher-strike call to reduce cost basis
- Example: Apple $107.50 call for $10, sell $120 call for $3.50 → risk drops to $6.50, breakeven from $117.50 to $114

### Bear Put Spreads
- Buy a put + sell a lower-strike put to reduce risk
- Example: Buy $170 put for $15, sell $150 put for $4 → risk drops from $15 to $11

### Iron Condors (Net Credit)
- Take premium upfront, risk defined amount ($5K-$10K)
- Grab targeted net credit early — DON'T ride to Friday expiration

### Rental Income (Covered Calls)
- Sell calls against shares you own like collecting rent
- Example: 500 shares Google → sell five $113 calls → $665 weekly "rent"

### Paid for Waiting (Cash-Secured Puts)
- Sell a put to agree to buy stock at discount, get paid cash upfront
- If stock never drops → keep cash, never buy

### The "Focus" Strategy
- Limit to 3-5 core stocks (SPY, Amazon, Tesla) instead of scanning thousands
- Same plays, same charts → bypass analysis from scratch every time

## 9. Advanced Risk Management (From Updated Sources)

### Sector Mirroring / Sympathy Plays
- Spot breakout in one company → find competitor in same sector that hasn't popped
- Example: TCAT explodes $5→$74 → play DLPN for $8→$10 pre-market spike

### The 3-Part Exit Plan (BEFORE every day trade)
1. **Exit Strategy:** Where you cut losses
2. **Repair Strategy:** Which option to sell to convert naked position into spread
3. **Reversal Strategy:** Which opposite-direction option to buy if market flips

### Scaling In By Halves
- Assume first entry point is wrong price
- Buy only HALF your contracts initially
- If stock drops → buy second half cheaper → easier breakeven on rebound

### Small Movement, High Volume Trading
- Don't search for $10-$20 movers
- Buy 1,000-2,000 shares of $5 stock → tiny 25-50 cent move = $500-$1,000 daily

### Scaling Up (Rolling Profits)
- Take smaller position → close for profit → reinvest capital + profit into double contracts for next leg

### Tax Carry-Forward Hack
- IRS allows $3K/year loss write-off
- $30K loss = write off $3K, carry forward $27K
- Use carry-forward to offset taxes on future mega-profits

### The 10 Cents + Commission Trap
- Don't refuse to close a 90%-up trade over $3 commission
- Friday news events can wipe out the entire 90% gain
