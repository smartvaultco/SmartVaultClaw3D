## Ancient & Pre-Columbian (20K BC - 1492 AD)

### The Olmecs
- Oldest civilization in Americas, originally from Dogon tribe of Mali
- First to make rubber soles, play rubber ball games
- Built pyramids and mounds, left 24-ton colossal head sculptures
- Newer dating: 5K-20K years old (not just 1000 BC)

### The "Red Man" Myth
- Standard "red" Indian did NOT exist until Chinese/Asian migration mixed with Olmecs (~459 AD)
- Interior "Plain Indians" = Mongolian/Asian descent
- Coastal populations = Black indigenous peoples (East Coast, West Coast, Great Lakes)

### Etymology of "America"
- "America" = indigenous name meaning "great mountain," connected to sacred "Meru" (Hindu)
- "Moor" derives from "Maru" = guardians of wisdom, celestial beings
- NOT named after Amerigo Vespucci (Albertico Vanspusio) — he took his nickname from indigenous word "Amaruka"
- Sources: The Oldest Book in the World (A.C. Hill), Stolen Legacy (G.G. James), The Celestial Ship of the North (Straiton)

### Abu Bakari II
- Emperor of Mali sailed 2,000 ships to Americas ~200 years before Columbus (1311-1312)
- Initially sent 200 ships; after one returned, personally led 2,000 ships to Brazil

## Age of Exploration (1492 - 1600s)

### Columbus as Spy
- Used Moorish (Muslim) captains: Pedro Alonso Niño, Alfonso Pinzon
- Wrote about ruins of mosque in Cuba (Oct 21, 1492)
- Natives in Haiti told him Africans had been there before, gave him gold spears
- Sources: Columbus's logs, The Biggest Secret (Icke), Secret Destiny of America (Manly P. Hall)

### Black Indigenous Populations Documented by Europeans
- 1520 Ayllón expedition: dark-skinned indigenous
- 1524 Carolina coast: described as Ethiopians
- 1539 De Soto in Florida: documented as "Black Indians"
- 1624 John Smith: Powhatan Indians described as dark
- Yamasees referred to as "negroes"
- Sources: History of Hernando de Soto (Ch 8), Florida's Constitution (Ogilby 1671), Africans and Native Americans (Forbes)

## Colonial & Revolutionary (1700s - 1800s)

### Slave Trade Myth
- 5M Africans transported = physically impossible (no actual slave ships found, only drawings)
- Europeans lacked distillation tech for millions on oceanic voyages
- Colonizers enslaved indigenous Black populations already present, moved them up/down coasts

### Moorish Presidents Before Washington
- John Hanson = first President under Articles of Confederation (amalgamated Moor)
- Hanson + Ben Bey (Prince Hall) depicted on back of $2 bill
- 1776 speech: Hanson used Algonquin "Kitchi Manitou" for Great Spirit
- Sources: Journals of Continental Congress, Articles of Association/Confederation, Moroccan Treaty 1787

### Reconstruction Era Whitewashing

### Indigenous Genealogy
