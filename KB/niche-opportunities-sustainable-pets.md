## NICHE 1: Sustainable & Eco-Friendly Products

### Market Size
- General sustainable: $355.3B (2024) → $692B by 2033 (CAGR 7.7%)
- Green tech: $25.47B (2025) → $73.90B by 2030 (CAGR 23.7%)
- Eco-friendly packaging: $256B (2025) → $369B (2030)
- 49% of Americans purchased eco-friendly product (March 2025, up from 43%)
- 23.8% of CPG market share, drove 41% of all CPG growth 2013-2024

### Demographics
- Dominated by Millennials + Gen Z, 56% female, 44% college degree+
- 80% willing to pay average 9.7% premium for eco-friendly goods
- Driven by eco-anxiety, guilt, value-alignment, skepticism of greenwashing

### Monetization Models
- Reusable/refillable goods (cups, beeswax wraps, cleaning supplies)
- Upcycled/reclaimed products (denim bags, reclaimed wood decor)
- Circular economy services: Product-as-a-Service (lease baby gear/tools), repair, buy-back
- DTC via Shopify, retail arbitrage (curated thrifting), Etsy, subscription boxes, refill subscriptions, paid communities

### Gaps & Opportunities
- Localized circular services (repair/rental/refilleries)
- Hyper-niche: pet burial, art supplies, compostable party decor
- Trust/transparency consulting
- SATURATED: generic straws, tote bags

### Red Flags
- Greenwashing = primary risk (regulatory fines + loss of trust)
- Complex supply chains, expensive certifications

## NICHE 2: Hyper-Personalized Pet Supplies

### Market Size
- Global pet care: $259.37B (2024) → $427.75B by 2032 (CAGR 6.60%)
- Pet accessories: $25.1B (2025) → $49.6B by 2034 (CAGR 7.9%)
- Personalized dog food: $143M (2024) → $282M by 2031 (CAGR 10.3%)
- Pet supplements: projected >$1.05B by 2027
- Chewy captures 48.35% of paid search clicks

### Demographics
- Millennials + Gen Z = largest pet-owning generations, 55% female, urban renters
- 41% increase in pet product spending 2024
- 48% prioritize pets' well-being above friendships/finances/careers
- 77% willing to pay more for healthy pet food
- 61% Gen Z + 59% Millennials use subscription services

### Monetization Models
- Print-on-Demand (POD): custom bowls, beds, hoodies, blankets (zero inventory risk)
- Customized nutrition/wellness: human-grade bespoke food subscriptions, personalized supplements
- Subscriptions: consumables auto-ship, curated boxes, "build-a-box," themed monthly kits
- Digital products: personalized pet portraits, breed-specific training guides

### Gaps & Opportunities
- Sustainable pet products (huge gap)
- High-end gift services (birthdays/memorials)
- Products targeting CATS, birds, small animals (most personalization = dogs only)
- SATURATED: low-quality POD items

### Red Flags
- Product safety paramount (non-toxic = non-negotiable)
- Strict regulations (FDA, AAFCO) for food/supplements
- Unreliable POD suppliers, high customer acquisition costs
